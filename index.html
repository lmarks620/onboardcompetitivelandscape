Where SharePoint Fundamentally Fails for Boards
Director access
Requires a Microsoft account + MFA on an employee platform. Directors at financial institutions are routinely blocked by their own company's firewalls — admin defaults to sending everything by email instead.
⚠ Critical (Aug 31, 2026)
Microsoft is retiring One-Time Passcode (OTP) auth for external users — a common workaround that disappears entirely.
Board book assembly
Manual — uploading, version control, folder management. ~4 hours per agenda cycle.
Audit trail ends at download
No way to know if a director printed, forwarded, or screenshotted a document. Standard M365 retains logs for only 90–180 days.
Copilot risk
Board materials in SharePoint are reachable by Copilot — confidential content (executive comp, acquisitions) can surface to any employee through a casual query.
No governance features
No native voting, consent agendas, e-signatures, meeting minutes workflow, D&O questionnaires, or board assessments.
Compliance burden stays with IT
Custom guest account management + quarterly permission audits required indefinitely.
Unmanaged device gap
Can't remote wipe a director's personal device when they roll off the board. No control over what happens after download.
Admin overhead

The Three Security Arguments for Decision-Makers
🤖 The Copilot Exposure (Most Urgent)

Copilot respects SharePoint permissions — so every permission mistake becomes instantly exploitable through conversational AI. Before Copilot, overly-permissioned users might never stumble on a board memo. With Copilot, they can ask for it by topic.

  • Gartner: SharePoint oversharing = #1 Copilot risk (March 2026)
  • Jan 2026: Microsoft DLP bug let Copilot bypass confidentiality policies — even for correctly configured tenants (advisory CW1226324)
  • US House of Representatives banned Copilot for congressional staff (March 2024)
🔐 The Permission Sprawl Problem

Permissions managed at file/folder level create broken inheritance, orphaned access, and overly broad sharing groups — a web even IT can't fully map.

  • 77% of IT professionals say current tools are insufficient for SharePoint permission visibility (Orchestry)
  • One admin reported 2,500+ sharing links for a single site
  • Microsoft built remediation tools (SharePoint Advanced Management, EEEU reports) to help find overshared sites — an implicit admission the native model fails
📱 The Unmanaged Device Gap

Board directors access materials on personal iPads, laptops, and phones. SharePoint cannot remote wipe a personal device, prevent a PDF from being forwarded via personal email, track whether a director printed board materials, or prevent screenshots.

"When a director rolls off your board, how do you ensure they no longer have access to materials they've already downloaded?" — Most organizations have no answer.

"Our board members are used to SharePoint"

"We already use Copilot for board prep"

"That's a great start. Copilot gives you a transcript, not a minutes draft — no governance-ready format, no structured action items. And if board materials stay in SharePoint, Copilot can surface confidential content to anyone with permissions. OnBoard's AI is isolated to the board environment — it can't be reached by enterprise AI tools."

Position IT as an ally: "Do they want to maintain a custom SharePoint config, manage external guest accounts, troubleshoot director logins, and audit permissions quarterly? Or hand that to a purpose-built platform and take it off their plate?" IT is usually OnBoard's strongest internal ally.

Don't challenge. Expand scope: "How do you handle it when a document changes after the packet goes out? How do you track whether directors actually read the materials? How do you manage action items between meetings?"
